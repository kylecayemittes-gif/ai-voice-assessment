## PART 1: WHO ARE YOUR CUSTOMERS? (THE ICPs)

### What's an ICP?

**ICP = Ideal Customer Profile**

It's basically a description of "the perfect customer we want to sell to." Think of it like a dating profile, but for businesses.

You have **6 different ICPs** to care about:
- **3 in Business**
- **3 in Government**

## BUSINESS CUSTOMERS (3 Types)

### Business ICP #1: Training Companies That Sell Courses

**Who they are:**
- Coding bootcamps (like General Assembly)
- Professional training companies (like project management certification programs)
- Companies that sell training to other companies

**Real examples from our research:**
- General Assembly - teaches coding
- Generation: You Employed - teaches workforce skills globally
- National Outdoor Leadership School (NOLS) - outdoor education

**How they make money:**
1. Students pay tuition OR companies pay for employee training
2. Students need to finish the course (completion = good reputation)
3. Students need to get jobs (job placement = marketing gold)

**Their biggest problem:**
Most online courses have **terrible completion rates** - only 20-30% of students finish (Source: 360Learning research).

Why? Because online learning is boring, students get stuck, and there's no community support.

**What this means for Canvas Career:**
If we can help them get completion rates from 30% to 75%, they'll make way more money and reputation. That's our value proposition.

**McKinsey Quote:**
> "Veterinary medicine has a shortage of doctors and nurses, so we often hire recently graduated students. LMS platforms that have learning pathways help us upskill these entry-level professionals."

**Translation:** They need to quickly train brand-new graduates to be job-ready. They need clear training paths, not just random courses.

**NRR = Net Revenue Retention**

Simple formula: If you had $100 last year from existing customers, how much do you have this year?
- **100% NRR** = You kept all $100
- **106% NRR** = You now have $106 (they bought more stuff or expanded)
- **<100% NRR** = Customers canceled or shrunk (bad)

**106% is AMAZING** because it means these customers love us so much they're spending MORE every year.

**Translation:**
